Information on Police Station:

The state of Gujarat came into existence as a new state on 1st May 1960, separated from the state of Greater Mumbai (Maharashtra) and with the existence of a separate state, the Gujarat Police also functioned with a new profile and headed by I.G.P. rank officer was appointed. Later in the year 1982, this post was upgraded and a new post of Director General of Police and Chief Police Officer was nominated.

Responsibilities of DG & IGP :

A Director General of Police (Law and Order) has been appointed to maintain law and order in the office of the Director General of Police. Whose main function is to monitor and carry out all matters related to law and order as per the guidance of the Director General of Police. Apart from this, DIGP Shri (Law & Order) has been appointed who performs law and order and crime related operations under the guidance of Director General of Police (Law & Order).

Commissionerate Area

1.Ahmedabad City 2.Vadodara City 3.Rajkot City 4.Surat City There are four commissionerate areas. Cities which come under the jurisdiction of Police Commissioner.

Range Area

There are total nine ranges namely Ahmedabad, Gandhinagar, Vadodara, Surat, Rajkot, Junagadh, Frontier Range, Panchmahal-Godhra and Bhavnagar.

District

There are 36 districts including Western Railway, Vadodara and Ahmedabad in the state of Gujarat. These districts are under the jurisdiction of Superintendent of Police.
